## Program Overview

Field ambassadors visit local businesses to get permission to place GasCap™ QR
placard displays. Each placard has a unique tracking code so scans, app opens,
and sign-ups are attributed back to the specific placement — and to the
ambassador who placed it.

**Goal:** Get QR placards into high-traffic automotive and driver-facing
locations across target markets.

## Who to Recruit

**Best candidates (ranked):**
1. Rideshare & delivery drivers (Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, Amazon Flex)
   — They ARE the target user. Authentic pitch. Already visit target locations.
2. Side-hustle seekers in local Facebook groups
3. College students near commercial corridors
4. Recently retired / semi-retired adults (flexible hours, reliable)
5. Military spouses (organized, flexible schedule needs)
6. Part-time real estate agents or insurance reps (comfortable cold pitching)

**Where to find them:**
- Facebook Groups: local DoorDash/Uber driver groups, side hustle groups
- Nextdoor (local, trusted, free)
- Craigslist → Gigs section
- Reddit: r/UberDrivers, r/doordash_drivers, r/sidehustle
- College campus job boards (Handshake)

**Green flags:** Drives constantly, has reliable car, comfortable with
strangers, organized enough to submit a Google Form.

**Red flags:** No car, wants guaranteed hourly pay, can't explain the app
after you explain it to them, overpromises.

## Pre-Deployment Prep (Before Kit Ships)

1. Open **gascap.app/admin/campaigns**
2. Pre-create 15–20 placements with placeholder names:
   ```
   ORL001P — "TBD — Pump"
   ORL002C — "TBD — Counter"
   ORL003W — "TBD — Window"
   ```
3. Code naming convention: `[CITY3][NUM3][TYPE1]`
   - P = Pump, C = Counter, W = Window, F = Flyer
   - e.g. ORL001P = Orlando, location 1, Pump
4. Print + laminate placards (code printed small on the back)
5. Attach magnets to pump placards
6. Assemble tent cards, cut window clings
7. Pack into labeled ambassador kit

> *"Hi, my name is [Name] — I work with GasCap, a free fuel calculator app

| "Need to check with corporate" | "No problem — here's a card. I can come back or follow up by email." |
| "We don't allow advertising" | "Totally understand. Thanks for your time." |

## Post-Placement Reporting (Google Form)

Ambassador submits immediately after each placement:

**Form fields:**
- Placard code (written on back of placard)
- Business name
- Address
- Owner/manager name + phone + email
- Placement type (dropdown)
- Photo upload (proof of placement)
- Consent form signed? (Yes/No)
- Notes

**Google Sheet** auto-populates from form submissions (master placement log).

## Ongoing Management

**Weekly (5 min):**
- Review scan activity per placement in admin dashboard
- Flag placards with zero scans for 2+ weeks (may have been removed)
- Ambassador checks flagged locations on next visit

**Monthly:**
- Pull ambassador stats from master placement log
- Calculate gas card rewards by tier
- Distribute rewards via TangoCard
- Send ambassadors monthly performance summary via GHL

**Quarterly:**
- Analyze which location types convert best
- Double down on top-performing categories
- Re-pitch locations that previously declined

## Placard Formats

**Tent card** — folded card stock, stands like an inverted V on a counter.
Standard size: 4"×6" folded. Front: headline + QR code. Back: 3 bullet points.

**Pump placard** — laminated 4"×6" card with 4 magnets on the back corners.
Weatherproof. Attaches to metal pump cabinet.

**Window cling** — printed vinyl, static cling, no adhesive needed.
Standard size: 4"×4" or 4"×6". For smooth glass surfaces.

## Bilingual QR Codes

Each placard can carry two QR codes side by side:
- English: `gascap.app/q/ORL001P`
- Spanish: `gascap.app/q/ORL001P?lang=es`

Both track under the same placement code. The admin dashboard shows EN vs.
ES scan and sign-up split per location — useful for Spanish-dominant areas.
