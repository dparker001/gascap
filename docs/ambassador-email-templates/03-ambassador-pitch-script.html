Hi {{contact.first_name}},

This is Email 2 of your ambassador onboarding. Here's the exact script to use when you walk into a business — plus how to handle the most common objections.

The Pitch (word for word)

"Hi, my name is [Your Name] — I work with GasCap, a free fuel calculator app for drivers. We're placing small QR code displays at local businesses so customers can calculate exactly how much gas they need before heading to the pump. There's no cost to you, it takes 30 seconds to set up, and it's a nice value-add for your customers. Would you be open to hosting one?"

That's it. Short, clear, and low-pressure. If they say yes, place the placard and hand them the consent form to sign.

Handling Objections

If they say…You say…
"What does it cost?"Nothing — completely free for you.
"What do you get out of it?"We're trying to get the word out. You'd be listed as a participating location in the app.
"Need to check with corporate"No problem — here's a card. I can come back or follow up by email.
"We don't allow advertising"Totally understand. Thanks for your time.

Best Locations to Target

  • Gas station pump areas (laminated card w/ magnets)
  • Gas station counter / register (tent card)
  • Oil change shop waiting room (tent card)
  • Tire shop waiting room (tent card + wall mount)
  • Car wash entrance (window cling)
  • Mechanic shop service counter (tent card)
  • Convenience store register (counter tent card)
  • Used car lot sales desk (tent card)

🚗 Pro tip for gig drivers: If you drive for Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, or Amazon Flex — you already visit these locations constantly. Knock out 3–4 placements on your normal routes and you're at the Builder tier ($50/month) in no time.
